Game recaps

Hawks 109, Knicks 108 — Game 3

CJ McCollum did it again. For the second straight game, Atlanta's midseason acquisition hit the decisive bucket in the closing seconds — this time a fadeaway jumper from 15 feet with 12.5 seconds left — to give the Hawks a 109-108 victory and a 2-1 series lead over the Knicks. Atlanta jumped out to an 18-point first-half lead behind McCollum's hot start (16 points by halftime) and a pair of early three-pointers from Jonathan Kuminga, sending State Farm Arena into a frenzy. The Knicks clawed back with a furious 23-9 fourth-quarter run — OG Anunoby (29 pts) and Miles McBride hitting big triples — and Jalen Brunson's three-point play gave New York a 108-105 edge with one minute left. But Jalen Johnson answered with a layup-in off a stripped shot, and then, with 12.5 seconds on the clock and a one-point deficit, McCollum calmly hit the go-ahead midrange. Jonathan Kuminga (21 pts, crucial late steal) sealed it on the final possession, stripping Brunson to end the threat. Atlanta held New York to just 15 fourth-quarter points on 5-for-22 shooting.

Raptors 126, Cavaliers 104 — Game 3

Toronto erupted for a historic second half, outscoring Cleveland 47-23 over the final two quarters to snap a 12-game playoff losing streak against the Cavaliers. Scottie Barnes set career playoff highs with 33 points and 11 assists, while RJ Barrett matched him with a career playoff-high 33 points (6-for-8 from three). The game was tied 54-54 at halftime and remained tight through three quarters (83-81 Toronto), before the Raptors caught fire shooting 8-for-9 from three in the fourth, outscoring Cleveland 43-23. Jamison Battle — who had barely played all series — scored all 14 of his points in the fourth quarter on 4-for-4 shooting from deep. Rookie Collin Murray-Boyles contributed 22 points and 8 rebounds. Cleveland's James Harden (18 pts, 8 turnovers) and Donovan Mitchell (15 pts, 1-for-7 from three) were kept in check all night.

Timberwolves 113, Nuggets 96 — Game 3

Minnesota's defensive stranglehold on Denver reached new heights, limiting the Nuggets to just 11 first-quarter points — a franchise postseason record for fewest allowed — and building a lead they never relinquished, at times as large as 27. Without Aaron Gordon (calf), Denver's offensive engine sputtered badly, with Nikola Jokić (27 pts, 15 reb) shooting an inefficient 7-for-26 from the field. Ayo Dosunmu (25 pts, 9 ast) was the game's offensive star off the bench, while Jaden McDaniels posted a 20-point, 10-rebound double-double with his thunderous fourth-quarter dunk serving as the knockout blow. Minnesota held Denver to just 34.1% shooting — the Nuggets' worst field goal percentage of the entire season.
