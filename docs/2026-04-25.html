Game recaps

Celtics 108, 76ers 100 — Game 3

Jayson Tatum and Jaylen Brown each scored 25 points to lift Boston to a hard-fought 108-100 road victory in Philadelphia, reclaiming a 2-1 series lead. The 76ers — without Joel Embiid (appendectomy) for the third straight game — had every chance to seize control, as Tyrese Maxey poured in 32 points and briefly put Philadelphia ahead 85-84 in the fourth quarter. But Boston answered with an 8-0 run, and Tatum put the game away with a dagger 3-pointer with 26 seconds left, capping an 11-point fourth quarter from the Celtics' superstar. Payton Pritchard added a crucial 15 points on five triples off the bench, while Boston's bench outscored Philadelphia's 35-14. The Celtics have now wrestled back home-court advantage heading into Game 4 on Sunday.

Lakers 112, Rockets 108 (OT) — Game 3

In one of the most dramatic games of the first round, LeBron James delivered yet another signature playoff moment, stealing the ball and drilling a tying 3-pointer with 13 seconds left in regulation to force overtime — before the Lakers held on for a 112-108 win that put them up 3-0. Houston had led by as many as six points in the final 30 seconds of the fourth quarter, looking set to avoid elimination, but two costly turnovers and a bad foul opened the door for Los Angeles. LeBron finished with 29 points, 13 rebounds, 6 assists and 3 steals across 45 grueling minutes. Marcus Smart was equally critical with 21 points, 10 assists and 5 steals. For Houston — missing Kevin Durant (sprained ankle) for the third straight game — Alperen Sengun was magnificent with 33 points and 16 rebounds, while Amen Thompson posted 26-11. But the young Rockets simply ran out of time and couldn't convert down the stretch. No NBA team has ever come back from 0-3 (159-0 all-time).

Spurs 120, Trail Blazers 108 — Game 3

Without Victor Wembanyama (concussion protocol), San Antonio needed its young stars to step up — and Stephon Castle and Dylan Harper delivered an all-time performance. Castle scored a postseason career-high 33 points while rookie Harper matched his career best with 27 points and 10 rebounds. Trailing by 15 in the third quarter, the Spurs went on a 21-5 run to close the period, seized the lead heading into the fourth, and never looked back. Harper's 10 first-quarter points of the fourth sealed the comeback. Portland's Jrue Holiday was brilliant with 29 points on 10-of-15 shooting, but it wasn't enough. Castle and Harper became just the second duo aged 21 or under to each score 25+ points in the same playoff game, joining Kevin Durant and Russell Westbrook.
