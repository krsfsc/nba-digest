Game recaps

Magic 113, Pistons 105 — Game 3

The Orlando Magic took a 2-1 series lead over the top-seeded Detroit Pistons in a dramatic, momentum-swinging affair at the Kia Center. Paolo Banchero and Desmond Bane each scored 25 points — Bane torching Detroit's defense with seven three-pointers — while Wendell Carter Jr. hauled in 17 rebounds to anchor Orlando inside. The Pistons mounted a stunning 25-8 run to tie the game at 104-104 with three minutes left, fueled by Cade Cunningham's second-half surge (27 pts total) and Tobias Harris's clutch 23 points. But the Magic shut Detroit out the rest of the way, capped by a Banchero steal and a wild stepback three that hit the back of the rim, bounced straight up, and dropped through — a moment that felt like destiny for Orlando. The eighth-seeded Magic are now a win away from a shocking 3-1 lead over the East's best regular-season team.

Thunder 121, Suns 109 — Game 3

Shai Gilgeous-Alexander was simply unstoppable at Mortgage Matchup Center, dropping a playoff career-high 42 points on a jaw-dropping 15-of-18 shooting performance to push the defending champion Thunder to a commanding 3-0 series lead. SGA feasted from mid-range, hit seven of eight jump shots inside the arc, and added eight assists — carrying OKC despite the absence of Jalen Williams (hamstring). Devin Booker left briefly in the third quarter with an ankle injury and returned, finishing with just 16 points as the Thunder held him to a non-factor role. Dillon Brooks led Phoenix with 33 points and Jalen Green added 26, but they never had an answer for SGA's relentless mid-range attack. OKC goes for the sweep Monday.

Knicks 114, Hawks 98 — Game 4

After dropping two consecutive one-point heartbreakers, the New York Knicks responded with an emphatic blowout in Atlanta, evening the series at 2-2. Karl-Anthony Towns recorded the first postseason triple-double of his career — 20 points, 10 rebounds, 10 assists — while OG Anunoby delivered a masterful two-way performance with 22 points and 10 boards. New York led 68-44 at halftime and extended the cushion to 20-plus points by the fourth quarter, with 'Let's go Knicks' chants filling State Farm Arena. The Knicks locked down CJ McCollum, holding him scoreless from three after he had drilled nine threes in the first three games. Jalen Johnson was limited to 14 points as Atlanta shot just 24% from deep. Game 5 heads back to Madison Square Garden on Tuesday.

Timberwolves 112, Nuggets 96 — Game 4

Ayo Dosunmu delivered one of the most remarkable performances in recent playoff memory — 43 points on 13-of-17 shooting, 5-of-5 from three, and 12-of-12 from the free throw line in 42 minutes — setting a record for the second-most points ever scored by a reserve in an NBA playoff game. The heroics were necessary after the Wolves lost Donte DiVincenzo to a season-ending torn Achilles in the first quarter and star Anthony Edwards to a left knee hyperextension late in the second half. Minnesota trailed 54-50 at halftime, but Dosunmu's 27-point second half — including a dagger 27-foot pull-up three with 90 seconds left — fueled a dominant second half to push the Wolves to a 3-1 series lead. Nikola Jokic had a valiant 24-point, 15-rebound, 9-assist game for Denver, but it wasn't nearly enough. As of Sunday morning, Edwards has been diagnosed with a bone bruise and hyperextension — no ligament damage — but is expected to miss multiple weeks, almost certainly ending his involvement in this series.
