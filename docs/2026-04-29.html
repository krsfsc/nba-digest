Game recaps

Knicks 126, Hawks 97 — Game 5

Jalen Brunson was absolutely locked in from the opening tip, scoring 39 points on 15-of-23 shooting with eight assists and just one turnover to put the Knicks firmly in command of their first-round series. New York broke free early with a 9-0 run late in the first quarter to lead 35-22 after one, then extended the cushion to 64-48 at the half after shooting 58.5% from the field. The lead swelled to 32 in the second half as Brunson ignited a 12-0 fourth-quarter run that ended any Atlanta hopes. Karl-Anthony Towns contributed 16 points, 14 rebounds and six assists while OG Anunoby added 17 points and 10 boards. For Atlanta, Jalen Johnson had 18 points, 10 rebounds and six assists, but CJ McCollum — the hero of Games 2 and 3 — was held to just six points on 3-of-10 shooting. The Knicks outrebounded the Hawks 48-27 and hold a 3-2 series lead heading to Game 6 Thursday in Atlanta.

76ers 113, Celtics 97 — Game 5

Joel Embiid, just 19 days removed from an emergency appendectomy, delivered a vintage 33-point, 8-assist performance to stave off elimination and send the series back to Philadelphia. The Celtics led by as many as 13 in the third quarter, but Embiid took over in the post — he is 0-for-5 from three but relentless around the rim — and the Sixers used a stunning 28-11 fourth-quarter run to pull away. Tyrese Maxey added 25 points and 10 rebounds on 10-of-18 shooting, and Quentin Grimes delivered a career playoff-high 18 points off the bench. Boston's fourth quarter was historically cold: just 10 points on 3-of-22 shooting, and 14 consecutive missed field goals to close the game. Jayson Tatum led the Celtics with 24 points and 16 rebounds, but the supporting cast went ice cold when it mattered most. Boston still leads the series 3-2 with Game 6 Thursday in Philadelphia.

Spurs 114, Trail Blazers 95 — Game 5

Victor Wembanyama (17 pts, 14 reb, 6 blk) and De'Aaron Fox (21 pts, 9 ast) led San Antonio to a dominant closeout win, with the Spurs never trailing en route to their first playoff series victory since 2017. San Antonio jumped out to a 17-4 start fueled by Julian Champagnie's 5-of-7 three-point shooting (19 pts), and led by as many as 28. Portland made a late push, cutting the deficit to nine with eight minutes remaining, but Wembanyama — who became the first player with 10+ rebounds and 6+ blocks in consecutive playoff games since Hakeem Olajuwon in 1997 — stuffed the rally. The Spurs advance to face the winner of the Nuggets-Timberwolves series in the West Semis.
